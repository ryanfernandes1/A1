Project Idea “Music Market”

Overview

This project is a mobile application or website, serving the purpose of connecting millions of music creators and producers around the world to one service, that will be known as “Music Market”. The app will be a marketplace based on the trade of selling music, such as beats, instrumentals, lyrics, melodies and vocals. It involves an interaction of various users, from individuals looking to generate an income from selling their music and individuals who are looking to purchase music to add to their production. The project will encapsulate the processes behind the production of the app, the applications importance and the motivation behind it. Overall, the project ‘Music Market’ consists of the incorporation of buyers and/or sellers of music on a single platform to enhance the efficiency of the process.

Motivation

The idea for this project formed when my cousin and I were talking about purchasing some beats to rap over for our tracks. After doing some searching on SoundCloud and Youtube we realised it is difficult to purchase beats online. It usually involves a lot of back and forth messaging, signing of contracts, messy bank transfers and overall takes too long. Most of the time, it is difficult to tell which beats are available, which are used why multiple users, etc. So, to solve this tedious process of trading music, an easily accessible mobile phone application such as ‘Music Market’ can be implemented to make everyone’s life a little easier.

Description

The app will not be very complex in nature, to ensure the engagement of users through simplicity. Firstly, as most applications incorporate these days, a login is required. So, the application should ask to either ‘log in’ or ‘create an account’, which can be done through an email ID and password. The application or website has to be able to remember accounts and stay logged in, if the user wishes, to stay with the theme of simplicity through usability.

Once an individual opens the application and is logged in, they will be taken to the home page. The home page will be known as the ‘browse’ page. This is where all the music for sale will be stored, it will be broken up into different sections such as: beats, lyrics, vocals, ad-libs, etc. The beats section will be separated into different genres and tempo of music so that users can be specific within browsing or explore various different sounds. This is obviously where users can preview the music briefly to see what they can purchase for their production.

The bottom of the application will be divided into three interactive sections, the middle being the home page, the right one being the account page and the left one being the history page.

The account page will be used for customising the account, including user-name, passwords, profile picture, payment methods and privacy. However, the essential use of the account page will be for the user’s music library, including their collection of all their music for sale and music purchased. Each piece of music, or ‘product’ will be listed separately with its individual price listing. The music will have three main features next to it, either ‘purchased’ meaning it has been bought, ‘for sale’ meaning it is available to buy and ‘draft’ for any music that has not been released for sale. Once music is sold or purchased, it is shown or will be updated to its current status within the account page. For exporting out of the app, music can be downloaded once shown here.

The page on the complete left from the ‘page options’ on the bottom of the screen will be the history page. This page is mainly utilised for viewing and keeping record of any sales or purchases made by a user within the application. This page can also be utilised for viewing music that has been viewed previously by the user, incase they wish to re-visit the music.

In the top right corner of the application will be the ‘chat’ option. This is where users can interact with each other from across the globe through messaging, incase any further details about the music are required to be discussed.

In the top left corner of the application will be the ‘menu’ option, this includes the settings for the application. This could encompass a variety of options, such as favourites and specific filters.

To tackle the issue of music being sold multiple times to various users, the price can be negotiated within the chat option, to a point where the music can be sold at an increased amount for exclusivity of the music. This is where both the buyer and seller mutually agree on a price for the rights of the music to be utilised only by the buyer. In regard to any profits made from any purchased music from the app, nothing will be owed to the seller unless a percentage of revenue for the music is formerly and mutually agreed upon by both the buyer and seller.

For payment options, once any piece of music is selected, it must be added to the ‘shopping cart’. This is where a review of the products selected will be displayed, accompanied by payment options available to purchase the music. So for any seller of music, their payment details will be saved for incoming money after a sale, and for any buyer of music their payment details may be saved or input for the transfer of money to the seller. This can be done through various options such as: Shop pay, PayPal, Apple Pay, AfterPay and Klarna.

Tools and Technologies

The software required to develop the application would of course be the coding of the application, which is the computer language of the application os that it can be utilised across multiple devices. The hardware required to create the application would be a computer system with servers to store all the application’s code and information.

Skills Required

Firstly, a project team is required to ensure the application is running smoothly. A manager will be required for the project team to ensure the users can communicate to the developer. User interface skills are also required to ensure the application is as easy as possible to utilise. Design skills are required to ensure the application is engaging to users so that the user levels are high and maintained. Communication skills are essential within the team to ensure any issues are resolved immediately. Coding languages such as HTML, C++ and Java are required to create the application and to communicate between one creator to another. Another essential skill for the application is security, this is so that any information from users are not illegally spread and shared. These skills are very reasonable within the field of IT, so it will not be difficult to either develop the skills through courses or teachers/tutors.

Outcome

If the project ‘Music Market’ was running and successful, then the millions of music creators around the world could purchase musical elements to improve their music at any time and anywhere. It would be much more efficient to purchase and sell music once it is all on the same platform and is clearly labelled for all users. The app would save a lot of time for users as once the music is posted for sale, it can be purchased and be ready for use almost instantly with the features of the application. The app could change the way music is sampled and created forever if it were this easy to connect sounds from all over the globe so efficiently in one place, which would increase the rate and volume of which music is created at.
